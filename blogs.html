Swarnagiri Sree Venkateswara Swami Devasthanam

Visited the breathtaking Swarnagiri Sree Venkateswara Swami Devasthanam in Bhuvanagiri—an architectural blend of Pallava, Vijayanagara, Chola, and Chalukya styles set across 22 serene acres. Highlights included the 12‑foot Sri Venkateswara idol, the 40‑foot Hanuman at the Hunumana Mandapa, and the majestic bronze bell said to be among India’s largest. A peaceful day of darshan and photography, with timings that make it perfect for a one‑day trip from Hyderabad.

Lalbaugcha Raja (The King of Lalbaug)

Paid a devotional visit to Lalbaugcha Raja—the iconic “King of Lalbaug” Ganesh pandal at Putlabai Chawl, drawing millions during Ganeshotsav since 1934. Experienced the vibrant aartis and the famed darshan queues, including the Navsachi and Mukh darshan that devotees line up hours for. Located at Lalbaug Market on GD Ambedkar Road, the mandal’s timeless idol and grand decor make it a must-see when in Mumbai.

Taj Mahal Palace Hotel

Captured the timeless grandeur of the Taj Mahal Palace in Colaba, Mumbai—India’s heritage 5‑star icon overlooking the Gateway of India since 1903. Built by Jamsetji Tata, the red‑domed Indo‑Saracenic landmark blends luxury with history and anchors the city’s waterfront skyline. A symbol of resilience and hospitality, it remains one of Mumbai’s most storied addresses and a must‑see on any city walk.

UTM lake view point

A breezy escape to UTM Lake View Point near Yadgarpalle—calm waters ringed by green hills and stormy monsoon skies, just about 40 km from Hyderabad. Perfect for quiet sunsets, pictures, and lakefront walks; facilities are minimal, so it stays peaceful and close to nature. One of those hidden-gem spots that locals call “Under The Moon Lake,” ideal for a quick getaway from the city.

Rajiv Gandhi International Cricket Stadium

At the Rajiv Gandhi International Cricket Stadium, Uppal—home of Sunrisers Hyderabad—soaking in the electric crowd and sweeping oval views. The venue hosts Tests, ODIs, T20Is, and IPL finals, with capacities quoted between about 39,200 and 55,000 across sources. Opened in the mid‑2000s and run by the Hyderabad Cricket Association, it’s one of India’s premier cricket cauldrons for day‑night matches.

ISKCON-Sri Sri Radha Madanmohan Mandir

Visited the ISKCON Sri Sri Radha Madan Mohan Temple in Abids, a serene Vaishnavite hub with deities of Radha‑Krishna, Gaura‑Nitai, and Jagannath‑Baladeva‑Subhadra. Located on Nampally Station Road near Pulla Reddy Sweets, the temple hosts daily aartis from early morning to evening and welcomes visitors free of charge. Peaceful darshan, devotional kirtans, and a vegetarian prasadam restaurant make it a refreshing spiritual stop in central Hyderabad.

Shri Jagannatha Swami Temple

Explored the Shri Jagannath Temple in Banjara Hills—built in classic Kalinga style with a soaring 70‑foot shikhara carved from red sandstone. Dedicated to Lord Jagannath with shrines for Balabhadra and Subhadra, it mirrors Puri’s sanctum layout and hosts the grand Rath Yatra each year. Open daily roughly 6:00 AM–12:00 PM and 5:00 PM–9:00 PM, it’s a serene spiritual stop in central Hyderabad.
